## Story structure (chapter highlights)
1. Product Quantity Overview  
   - Shows count/quantity of items sold by category. Caption notes Office (Accessories & Organization) sells the most products.

2. Profitability by Category (over time)  
   - Line charts by year. Annotation highlights Printing & Technology profit increased ~64.5% over four years.

3. Profit by Product  
   - Bar chart of product-level profit. Annotation calls out the graphing calculator as the top contributor (~43% of total profit).

4. Customer segmentation & map for top product  
   - Dashboard showing buyer segments and geographic distribution for the graphing calculator. Individual consumers are the largest buyer segment.

---

## Key insights
- Printing & Technology has shown strong profit growth (~64.5% over four years) despite not having the highest quantity sold.
- One product (graphing calculator) drives a large share of profit (~43%), indicating concentration risk and opportunity.
- Individual consumers are the main buyers for the top product, with geographic clusters that suggest targeted regional demand.

---

## Business recommendations
- Investigate pricing, supply, and inventory risks for the graphing calculator due to profit concentration.  
- Consider marketing or diversification strategies to reduce concentration risk and grow other profitable products.  
- Use geographic demand signals for targeted promotions and inventory allocation.

---

## How this was built (high level)
- Prepared and filtered the retail dataset (order-level fields: product, category, sales, profit, dates, customer segment, region).  
- Created separate worksheets:
  - Quantity by category/product (sorted bar chart)
  - Profit by category over years (line chart)
  - Profit by product (ranked bar chart)
  - Customer segment + map (dashboard)
- Assembled the worksheets into a Tableau story, added captions and annotations to guide the narrative.  
- Published to Tableau Public as `Story1`.
